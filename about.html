My wife and I live in the Dallas, TX area without dogs, although we would like to rescue a Greyhound one day. While she's pursuing her MFA at UNT, we're both making stuff for our shop, The Happy Factory. I'm also the curator of Can you define love?, a blog about people and their thoughts on love.

I'm currently reading Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea by Jules Verne. My favorite colors are cerulean and hot pink with glitter.

Professionally

I’m working at Kalkomey as a UX Designer with a small team of smart people who are leading the world in providing recreational safety materials. The UX team designs and builds the online versions of the safety courses and e-publications, and we also interact with other teams, such as customer service and marketing. You know, everything that involves user experience.

Before Kalkomey, I was a design strategist and support specialist at Unit Interactive, where I worked with a broad-range of clients like Woot!, John Degroote, and Peeper’s. Also, as the customer support specialist, a huge part of my job was customer support for Unify.

My Tools

Generally I use Photoshop for quick design comps, then get into the browser as soon as possible. Using Sublime Text 2 + iA Writer + Dropbox to sync everything across devices, except my phone. I do not have a smartphone, I have an indescructible Nokia from 2002.

The technologies I use when building the web include HTML, CSS, some JavaScript (still learning it), and perhaps Wordpress, Twitter Bootstrap, and Gridset, depending on the project. I'm also familiar with CodeIgniter, and I'm currently learning Ruby on Rails and Git since joining Kalkomey.

As for making things outside of code or writing, I draw lettery things, screenprint, sculpt, make books, and developing my illustration skills.

This Site

Previously, my personal site has been built using Smarty, Wordpress, static pages for use with Unify, and now it's built with Jekyll, a static site generator. For the underlying grid, I'm using the fantastic Gridset by the team at Mark Boulton Design.

While the fonts may vary between case studies, the primary font is Bitter, hosted by Google.
