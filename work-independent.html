  • letterforming

    I’ve been into lettering and calligraphy since about sophomore of college when I was cheap and didn’t understand the value of solid typefaces and well-constructed fonts.

    Until recently I’ve only worked in two-dimensions, but discovering that third one has been a carousel of emotions. Check out more lettering on Dribbble. Or don’t, whatever.

  • North Point Christian Church

    North Point is a small, simple church in Old Town Lewisville, TX. Before the rebrand, there were a handful of inconsistent logos, which didn’t match the spirit of the church family, and the site had the same problems.

    I have strong feelings on designing for churches, but in this case the work was necessary. This logo was listed on Church Relevance as a top church logo which is a thing apparently.

  • Read-Rite Market

    The Read-Rite Market was an all-day event celebrating the written word the social practice of reading. My wife and I worked with Oil and Cotton to create a logo for the occasion, and we also live-printed the official posters.

    Also, not long ago my wife and I rebranded/renamed ourselves and are now known as Various Mfg Co; formerly we were known as We Create Happy. (New site coming soon.)

  • Rachel Rushing

    Rachel Rushing is an artist who works in photography, installation, book arts, and whatever else she can think of. She’s also a speaker, and has done several workshops on various alternative process photography techniques.

    She is beautiful and funny, and I love her. We’ve been married since May 23, 2010.

  • e3 Expeditions

    Between working at Unit and Kalkomey, I worked on a small freelance project with the team at e3 Partners. They needed some responsive design expertise, and this project was a great place for them to learn.

    The microsite for e3 Expeditions was designed by an old friend, Jeremy Honea, and I was the front-end developer on the project. This was built apart of a responsive design framework, like Foundation or Bootstrap, and before I knew of such things (back in 2012).
